Career Options Card Game

Educational Game

Career Options Card Game

Goal: Create an opportunity for sophomore and junior college students to explore careers they may have not considered.
Audience: Mid-level (sophomore & junior) college students
Method: Using career data from O*net Online I designed small cards with descriptions of jobs. Each card has the job title and a description of the common duties associated with the type of work. Each card also had symbols to indicate the industries where someone could find these types of jobs.
Life Extreme Card Game

Educational Game

Life Extremes Card Game

Goal: Enable conversations about purpose through the exploration of lifestyle choices and sacrifices.
Audience: Mid-level (sophomore & junior) college students
Method: Each card is a single color, red, purple, or green and states a potential life choice that someone may have to make. Each card is double sided which each choice representing both extremes on the spectrum which they represent. The colors represent different parts of life: red has relationship choices, green has work/career choices, and purple has general lifestyle choices.

Declare a Purpose, Not a Major


Goal: Introduce students to the major selection process and support them in identifying possible majors to explore.
Audience: First year college students
Method: This workshop was developed as part of new student orientation for first year students who were entering the college undeclared.
